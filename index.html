Metaphysics

What is metaphysics?

is the branch of philosophy that deals with the first principles of things, such as being, knowing, substance, cause, identity, time, and space, among other abstract notions..


Examples

  • Metaphysics What is existence and reality? What is the nature of space and time?
  • EpistemologyHow do we know what we know? Are the things that we know true?
  • Ethics What is the right course of action in the face of problems or dilemmas?
  • Logic What is truth? How can I say that my statements are true?
  • Aesthetic What is beauty? What is our basis of judgment to say that it is beautiful?

Additional info

  • Epistemology - The argument between rationalism and empiricism, or whether knowledge may be obtained a priori ( prior to) or posteriori ( posterior to), is a common topic in modern epistemology.
  • There are two types of knowing. Empiricism is the knowledge gained primarily through observation and experience. Meanwhile, moral rationalism, or ethical rationalism, is the view according to moral truths are knowable a priori by reason alone.

Additional info

  • There are 2 forms of forming knowledge. Propositional Knowledge - knowledge that some statements are either true or false. Propositional Knowledge - is derived from a variety of perspectives, this includes philosophy, history, literature, and science
  • Ethics - Meta-ethics focuses on understanding the nature and meaning of moral judgments, actions, and states of being, rather than determining if lying is always wrong, and aims to clarify what is right or wrong.

Additional info

  • Social and Political Philosophy delves into questions of justice, political authority, and individual rights within societal contexts, providing normative guidance for how societies should be organized. Influential thinkers like John Locke and John Rawls have shaped this field's discourse on the fundamental principles underlying social institutions.
  • Philosophy of science - is an inquiry into the nature, methods, and assumptions underlying scientific knowledge. It scrutinizes the fundamental principles guiding scientific investigation and aims to understand the nature of scientific reasoning and discovery.
